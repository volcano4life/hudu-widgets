Article Overview
When you log in to your work accounts, you are asked to prove who you are in two steps. The first step is your password. The second step — called two-factor authentication (2FA) — is an extra check that confirms it is really you logging in, even if someone else has stolen your password.
Our preferred method is a free app on your smartphone — simple, always with you, no physical device needed. However, we know that not everyone wants to use their personal phone for work, and we fully respect that. This article is for those staff members.

The app might actually suit you better
This guide covers our hardware security devices, and they work brilliantly. But if you have a smartphone available, a free authenticator app may be a simpler, more resilient choice. Here is why — honestly.
Authenticator App
Lives on your phone — always with you
  • Free — nothing to order, nothing to wait for
  • Nothing physical to lose, break, or forget at home
  • Moves to a new phone with you
  • No replacement process or service desk ticket needed
  • Works even if you change jobs — just removes the work account
Hardware Devices — Be Aware
Physical devices carry physical risks
  • Can be lost, left at home, or damaged
  • A lost or broken device means raising a ticket and waiting
  • Temporary workarounds may be needed while waiting
  • Replacement devices have a cost to the organisation
  • Code generator token batteries eventually run out
🔒
Worried about your personal phone or data? You do not need to be.
We completely understand that some people feel uncomfortable installing a work-related app on their personal phone. The good news is that an authenticator app does not give the organisation any access to your phone, your contacts, your location, your messages, or anything else personal. It is not a device management tool.
All it does is display a six-digit code — exactly the same as the FEITIAN token does — and it does not even need an internet connection to do so. There is nothing on your phone that your employer can see, track, or control. Your personal device remains entirely yours.

What does it look like?
The YubiKey is a small, flat device — roughly the size of a door key — that you can keep on your keyring or in your bag. It has a gold circle on one side (the touch sensor) and a USB-C connector on one end.
How does it work?
🔌 Plug it in
Insert the YubiKey into the USB-C port on your laptop or computer. When asked to confirm your identity, simply tap the gold circle with your finger. No code to type, no app to open.
📲 Tap it
On a modern phone or tablet, simply tap the YubiKey against the back of the device — the same way you would tap a contactless card.
Why it works well for our organisation
  • Nothing to forget — no battery to charge, no code to read, no clock to check
  • Works with all the systems we use, including Microsoft 365, email, and remote access
  • Built to last — waterproof, crush-resistant, and no moving parts
  • If lost, IT can disable it immediately and issue a replacement
  • Small enough to live on your keyring permanently
⚠
If you lose your YubiKey
Contact IT Support straight away. They will disable your device so nobody else can use it, and arrange for a replacement to be sent to you.

What does it look like?
The FEITIAN token is a small, keyring-sized device with a small screen and a single button on the front. It looks similar to the kind of security token you may have used with online banking.
How does it work?
1
Press the button on the front of the token
2
A six-digit number appears on the screen — this is your one-time code
3
Type that number into the login screen when asked for your 2FA code
4
The code changes automatically every 30 seconds, so each code can only ever be used once
Why it works well for our organisation
  • Works on any computer, including shared or locked-down machines where USB devices are not allowed
  • The code on the screen is large and easy to read
  • No software needs to be installed anywhere
  • The battery lasts for several years — a low-battery warning appears on screen when it needs replacing
  • Simple enough for anyone to use with no technical knowledge required

Do I need to carry my device with me at all times?
Yes. You will need your device every time you log in to your work accounts from a new device or after a period of inactivity. We recommend keeping it on your keyring so it is always with you.
What if I forget my device at home?
Contact IT Support. They can arrange temporary access for you while you retrieve your device. Please do not share your login with a colleague in the meantime — this puts the organisation’s data at risk.
Can someone else use my device to log in?
Not without also knowing your password. The device and your password work together — one is useless without the other. This is what makes two-factor authentication so much safer than a password alone.
Will my device ever expire?
The YubiKey does not expire and has no battery. The FEITIAN token has a battery that lasts several years — it will show a warning on screen when the battery is running low. Contact IT Support if you see this warning.
What if my device stops working?
Contact IT Support. Do not attempt to open or repair the device yourself.
Can I use my YubiKey for personal accounts too?
The YubiKey can be registered with personal accounts that support security keys (such as Google or Microsoft personal accounts), but this is entirely optional and not required by the organisation. Please do not register the device with anything that could compromise your work login.
